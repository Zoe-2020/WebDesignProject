Penguins

A Little Introduction...

All penguins live in the southern hemisphere, such as the continent of Antarctica. Penguins may be birds, but they cannot fly and instead of wings have flippers. They may not be able to fly but they are amazing swimmers reaching up to 15 miles per hour. Their unique coloured body works as camouflages when they are in the water to help hunt for their next meal which usually consist of "krill", small shrimplike animals.

Interesting Facts!

  • They can be from 38 to 107 centimeters in height
  • They can weight anything from 0.9 to 3.6 kilograms
  • Their scientific name is Spheniscidae
  • No penguins live at the North Pole
