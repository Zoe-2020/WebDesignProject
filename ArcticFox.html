ARCTIC FOX

A Little Introduction...

An arctic fox is a small fox with a body length of 46 to 68-centimetre native to the Arctic regions. They live in burrows and can survive temperatures as low as minus 58° Fahrenheit. Their white coat is a natural camouflage which changes with season turning to a grey to blend into rockier surrounding. Not only does their iconic white coat work as a defence mechanism it also allows the arctic fox to hunt effectively, with their diet consisting of small animals’ such as rodents, hares, birds, eggs and fish.

Interesting Facts!

  • They are around 28 centimeters in height
  • They can weight anything from 1.5 to 9 kilograms
  • Their thick tails not only help them balance but is also used for warmth in especially cold weather
  • Their scientific name is Vulpes Iagopus
